Chapter 01 / Mindset shift

From engineers to operators.

Daniel, Samuel — we should still use our engineering skills, but on operations, data, tracking, reporting, creator quality and campaign execution. Not on building a full app. The real risk right now is not lack of software. The real risk is no repeat paying businesses.

The brutal truth

A technical co-founder who helps me close, run, track and report campaigns for paying customers is more valuable right now than one who quietly builds features nobody has paid for. I'd rather have both of you in the trenches with me than at your keyboards.

Chapter 02 / The role split

Three founders. Three engines.

Here's how I'm proposing we split the work. Each of us owns one business engine. Nobody covers more than one. This is the only way to stop the three of us drifting into general-purpose busywork that doesn't move TrendStack forward.

Role 01 · Michael (me)

CEO.

Sales · Strategy
  • I close paying SMEs.
  • I pick the niche, set the menu, defend pricing.
  • I lead outreach and close paid pilots.
  • I control the campaign promise.
  • I own partnerships and repeat buyers.
Role 02 · Samuel

Creator Lead.

Creators · Campaigns
  • You recruit and vet 30–50 creators.
  • You run creator tiers & reliability scoring.
  • You own content review and revision flow.
  • You coordinate posting and proof.
  • You build the reliable creator supply system.
Role 03 · Daniel

Data Lead.

Tracking · Payouts · Reports
  • You run the single campaign tracker.
  • You maintain the creator database.
  • You own tracking, fraud checks, payout proof.
  • You deliver every report within 72 hours.
  • You build internal tooling — never the product.
Why I'm splitting it this way: creators are mobile-first — they live on WhatsApp, TikTok, Instagram. Samuel, you naturally understand user flow, friction and onboarding from your mobile work — that's why the creator side is yours. Daniel, you naturally understand structured data, tracking and reporting — that's why the operating system is yours. We both apply that instinct to operations first, not to a product nobody has paid for yet.
Chapter 03 / Role 01 · Me

CEO — find the buyer.

My focus is the thing that can kill us fastest: can we get Nigerian SMEs to pay us repeatedly? I won't spend most of my time managing creators — that's Samuel's seat. I'll spend it finding out whether businesses will actually pay.

A · NICHE

I pick the first vertical

Beauty, fashion, food, restaurants or startups. I pick one and defend it. I won't let us dilute the offer across all five.

B · PRICING

I set packages & price

I set the menu, hold the line on price, refuse what doesn't fit. I decide what we will and will not offer.

C · OUTREACH

I lead the sale

I speak to SME owners daily. Send proposals fast. Close paid pilots. This is the only activity that proves us.

D · PROMISE

I own the campaign promise

I control what we deliver. I protect us from bad campaigns and reject creators and briefs that don't fit.

E · REVIEW

I read every result

I sit with the business on every campaign report and decide what's next. I close the loop myself — I won't delegate the verdict to either of you.

F · REPEAT

I drive repeat business

I build case studies and push for repeat bookings. I decide which niche actually works once we have 5 paid campaigns of evidence.

Chapter 04 / Role 02 · Samuel

Creator & Campaign Lead.

Samuel — I want you to design the creator experience as if it were a mobile app, but execute it through WhatsApp, Forms, Sheets and Drive. Your job isn't "bring influencers." Your job is to build a reliable creator supply system. No actual app yet.

A · RECRUIT

Build the vetted bench

You bring in 30–50 creators in 21 days. For each one, capture niche, follower count, average views, audience, sample work, rate, reliability and fraud risk.

B · SCORE

Tier them

New · Verified · Trusted. You give new creators small fixed-fee gigs only. Better campaigns and small bonuses unlock at Trusted.

C · ONBOARD

Set the rules

You own the application form, welcome message, participation rules, content rules, payment expectations, disclosure reminders.

D · REVIEW

Run the 9-step loop

Brief → confirm → draft → review → approve / revise → post → proof → analytics → pay. Same loop, every campaign — you own it.

E · CHANNELS

Disciplined comms

One announcement channel. One campaign chat. One form. One tracker. I don't want one chaotic group where everyone talks.

F · IMPROVE

Drop & promote

You track dropout. Remove unreliable creators. Promote consistent ones into the Trusted tier. Tighten the pool every cycle.

Chapter 05 / Role 03 · Daniel

Data, tracking, payouts & reports.

Daniel — I don't want you building the full backend yet. I want you building the manual data infrastructure that lets us run campaigns without confusion. Every campaign tracked. Every creator logged. Every naira accounted for. Every result reportable. Every fraud signal noticed.

A · BUILD

Build the campaign tracker

One Sheet / Airtable. Business, package, budget, creators, status, payment, tracking link, results — all in one place. This is your single source of truth.

B · STRUCTURE

Own the creator database

Creator ID, niche, follower count, engagement, history, on-time score, fraud risk, tier, payment history. You're designing the future schema today — keep that in mind.

C · TRACK

Capture per-campaign signal

UTMs, Bitly, WhatsApp links, coupon codes, screenshots, manual enquiry logs. Credible — not perfect. Just enough to show ROI to the SME.

D · DETECT

Write the fraud rules

View spikes, generic comments, audience mismatch, post deletion, manipulated screenshots. Simple rules + manual review. No AI fraud detection yet — please.

E · PAY

Run the payout ledger

Fee, approval, bonus, due date, status, posting proof, screenshot proof, hold reason. Margin per campaign. No casual payments via random WhatsApp instruction — every payout goes through your ledger.

F · REPORT

Deliver the 72-hour report

Goal, posts, views, link clicks, coupon usage, orders / leads, best creator, next steps. One reusable template, fraud checklist, payout checklist. The SME-facing report is yours.

Boundaries

The next 90 days: nothing else.

Our engineering instinct will pull us back toward building. We need to resist it. Here's the line I'm drawing — what's forbidden, what we do by hand, and the small set of tools that are allowed.

Forbidden

Do not build.

  • A new mobile app.
  • The old campaign marketplace, rebuilt.
  • Complex dashboards or matching.
  • Automated payments or AI fraud detection.
  • Hundreds of creators recruited at once.
  • Anything that hides from the sales conversation.
Manual ops

Do by hand.

  • Creator vetting, onboarding, communication.
  • Brief writing, submission review, revisions.
  • Posting confirmation & screenshot proof.
  • Tracking via UTM, Bitly, coupons, manual logs.
  • Payout approval and ledger entry.
  • Campaign reports in Docs / Slides.
Lightweight tools

Allowed now.

  • Landing page for SME packages.
  • Creator application & business inquiry forms.
  • Campaign tracker & creator database in Sheets.
  • Payout ledger & report template.
  • Link tracking & coupon setup.
  • Internal campaign status dashboard.
Two rules

If it helps run paid campaigns this week, build it. If it is for a future scalable platform, delay it. Aim for 80% operations · 20% lightweight tools.

Chapter 06 / Cadence

90 days. Weekly. Then decide.

We run on a tight rhythm so none of us drifts. Four phases. One founders' meeting a week. One clear bar to clear before I let us go back to software.

01
Weeks 1–2 · Setup

We build the manual rails.

We build in parallel. Nothing is being sold yet — but every piece of infrastructure for our first sale gets laid down.

  • Me: finalize offer, pricing, prospect list. Start sales calls.
  • Samuel: recruit and vet 30–50 creators. Build the scoring sheet.
  • Daniel: build the campaign tracker, creator database, payout ledger, report template.
02
Weeks 3–6 · First sales

We close and deliver.

First paid pilots go live. Our operations get stress-tested. All three of us touch a real campaign.

  • Me: close paid pilots. Daily SME conversations. Defend pricing.
  • Samuel: match creators to campaigns. Review submissions. Track reliability.
  • Daniel: track progress. Monitor links and codes. Manage payouts. Prepare reports.
03
Weeks 7–10 · Repeat & refine

We push toward repeat.

Now we're optimizing. Case studies emerge. Bad creators get dropped. The pool tightens. The story sharpens.

  • Me: push for repeat campaigns. Build case studies. Decide which niche wins.
  • Samuel: improve the pool. Remove unreliable creators. Create the Trusted tier.
  • Daniel: improve fraud checks. Raise report quality. Analyze campaign margin.
04
Weeks 11–13 · Decide

We give the verdict.

The three of us sit down with the data. Continue, narrow, or change. We identify the top repeated pains worth automating.

  • We review results — paid campaigns, repeat customers, margin, creator quality.
  • We decide whether to continue the manual model.
  • We identify what, if anything, should become software.
  • We list the top repeated manual pains.

Weekly meeting

Ten questions. One hour.

One founders' meeting a week. We ask only these ten questions and skip everything else. It keeps us focused on business proof — not activity.

Founders standup

Run this top to bottom. Don't drift.

  1. How many businesses did we speak to?
  2. How many proposals did we send?
  3. How many paid campaigns did we close?
  4. Which creators are campaign-ready?
  5. Which creators failed or behaved badly?
  6. Which campaigns are currently active?
  7. Which reports are due?
  8. Did we make margin?
  9. Did any business ask to repeat?
  10. What manual process is painful enough to automate?
Return clause

When software is allowed back.

We only go back to building software when we can honestly say: "We've done this manually 10–20 times, and the same workflow keeps repeating." Until then, software is premature for us.

Green · build

10+ paid campaigns completed. 3+ repeat customers. A known winning segment. A stable workflow. A known report format.

Decision Start building the smallest piece of software that automates the most painful manual step. Nothing more.
Yellow · hold

Paid campaigns running, but margin or repeat is weak. Reports inconsistent. Some creators unreliable.

Decision Don't build yet. Fix pricing, tighten the bench, raise report quality. Re-check at 30 days.
Red · do not build

Interest without payment. Chaotic campaigns. No repeat. Margin unclear. Founders drifting.

Decision Stop. Re-scope the offer or the target customer. Software does not solve a missing buyer.

We earn the right to be engineers again.

Daniel, Samuel — we are not abandoning software. We are discovering what software is worth building. This manual phase is my version of research, sales validation, operational design and product discovery rolled into one. We run the playbook. We deliver strong reports. We find the repeat buyer. Then the three of us decide what software is actually needed.

Push back where you disagree before our next meeting,
— Michael
