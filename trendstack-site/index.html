Chapter 01 / Pivot Strategy

From open marketplace
to a service that actually ships.

We're moving from a broad creator marketplace into a managed campaign service for Nigerian SMEs. Creator supply has always been easier for us than paying demand. Our next milestone isn't 500 creators — it's 5–10 paid SME campaigns and 2–3 customers who book the next wave.

Was From
Is To
Open marketplace, anyone joins.
Invite-only execution with a small vetted bench.
Promoter creates the campaign and hopes.
We define the package, creator fit, plan and report upfront.
Payouts driven by raw views.
Fixed creator fees + capped bonuses on verifiable outcomes.
Software-first experience.
Manual-first service via WhatsApp, Sheets, Forms, Drive.
Broad promoter targeting.
Specific SME verticals: beauty, fashion, food, wellness, startups, events.
Creator growth as the main activity.
Paid campaigns and repeat demand as the main activity.
Brutal truth

Creator supply is easy. The hard part is closing 5–10 paid campaigns and earning 2–3 repeat customers. Everything in this playbook bends toward that.

Why this direction

Six reasons the pivot
makes sense.

01 / SIMPLICITY

SMEs want simplicity

Small businesses don't want to learn a platform. They want someone to manage creators, content, posting and results.

02 / AFFORDABILITY

Nano & micro is affordable

SMEs can afford smaller creators — especially when the offer is packaged and priced clearly upfront.

03 / TRUST

Trust is our product

We win by vetting creators, checking briefs, preventing budget waste and explaining what actually happened.

04 / LEARNING

Manual ops teach the truth

Running campaigns by hand reveals pricing, objections, creator problems, fraud and repeat demand — before software is built.

05 / RELEVANCE

Local context matters

A Lagos restaurant or fashion brand needs creators with local audience relevance — not follower count.

06 / REPEAT

Reports create repeat business

The final report shows content, engagement, leads, learnings and the next recommended campaign. Our repeat-sale engine.

We don't sell virality. We sell managed execution, vetted creator access, usable content, tracking — and a clear report.

Discovery questions.

Ask enough to understand the campaign — don't turn the call into an interrogation.

  1. What product, service or offer do you want to promote?
  2. Who is your target customer?
  3. Which city or audience location matters most?
  4. What action do you want — WhatsApp, order, booking, signup, store visit, follow?
  5. Have you used creators before? What worked, what failed?
  6. What is your current monthly marketing spend?
  7. Do you have product samples, photos, videos, landing pages?
  8. How quickly can you approve creator content?
  9. Can you track coupon codes, WhatsApp enquiries, bookings or sales?
  10. Prefer a small test, or a stronger 8–10 creator sprint?

Objections, handled.

We respond with confidence — never overpromise. These are the six objections every Nigerian SME will give.

01"Can we try it for free?" +
Response We don't run free campaigns — creators have to be paid and properly managed. We can start with a small Starter Sprint to reduce risk.
02"Can you guarantee sales?" +
Response No honest campaign partner can guarantee sales. We guarantee managed creator delivery, content review, posting proof, tracking and reporting.
03"Why not just DM influencers myself?" +
Response You can — but we save you sourcing, negotiation, briefing, chasing, review, payment coordination and reporting. That's where campaigns usually fail.
04"Your price is high." +
Response The price includes multiple creators, management, review, coordination, reporting and creator payouts. Cheaper usually means less control.
05"We only want big influencers." +
Response For SMEs, nano and micro creators give more targeted community trust and better affordability. Big influencers come later, once the motion is proven.
06"Will this go viral?" +
Response We don't promise virality. We promise quality creator content, local visibility, enquiries and campaign learnings — the foundation for the next wave.

Chapter 06 / The Verdict

Day 90.
Score the evidence.

We don't assume the model works. We score it. Green continues and standardizes. Yellow adjusts. Red simplifies — into UGC or a different customer, without writing software yet.

Green

5+ paid campaigns. 2+ repeat commitments. Margin above 20%. Creator chaos manageable. Reports understood.

Decision Continue. Standardize packages. Improve tools. Build only the most painful automation.
Yellow

Paid campaigns closed — but repeat demand or margin is weak.

Decision Adjust pricing. Reduce creator count. Improve reporting. Narrow the vertical focus until the green band is reached.
Red

Lots of interest, few payments. Campaigns too chaotic. Customers don't repeat.

Decision Simplify into UGC / content procurement, or change the target customer. Do not build software yet.

We earn the right to build software.

Software should support a proven operation — not hide uncertain demand, unclear pricing or weak repeat business. We run the manual playbook first. We build only what hurts to do by hand. Then we earn the right to scale.
