We are not selling cheap influence. We are selling managed creator content and campaign execution. The moment we sell "many influencers for ₦100k", the unit economics collapse on us.

Chapter 01 / Six findings

If you read nothing else.

Six things I want both of you to take away before we go deeper. Everything that follows in this memo is the math, evidence and examples that got me here.

01 · Creators will play

Yes — but only the right ones.

Platform monetization in Nigeria is patchy. Most nano and lower-micro creators are not earning meaningful platform money yet. They will take a fair, reliable ₦25k–₦80k gig from us. Established 100k+ creators won't, and we shouldn't chase them.

02 · ₦100k is not a campaign

It's a content test.

₦100k with 20% margin leaves ₦16k per creator across 5 creators. That buys us bad work and disappointed SMEs. ₦100k should only ever buy 1–2 creator videos — never a full campaign.

03 · Our floor is ₦250k

₦500k+ is the real sweet spot.

Below ₦250k–₦300k we can't pay creators properly, manage the campaign, track results, write the report, and still keep margin. ₦500k is where the model becomes repeatable.

04 · SMEs will pay

But not as an ad replacement.

They'll pay us because we give them creator content, local trust, execution and a report. Ads alone don't give them that. We position as complementary to TikTok/IG ads — not as a cheaper alternative.

05 · Fixed fees, no views

Pay creators on completion.

Fixed content fee + posting confirmation + capped sales/code bonus + repeat-creator bonus. No view-based payouts — that's where fraud lives. Product samples count for beauty / food / fashion.

06 · The pivot clause

If SMEs only have ₦100k, we pivot.

If our 30-SME test says budgets are stuck at ₦50k–₦100k, we don't force the influencer model. We pivot to UGC content for ads: creators make videos, the SME boosts them through TikTok / Meta ads. Cheaper for them, cleaner for us.

Chapter 02 / Will creators play?

Platform money doesn't kill us.

Samuel, this was your concern — and it's fair. Here's the honest picture I pieced together from public Nigerian-creator sources. Platform monetization raises the bar for what we have to offer creators. It doesn't take them off the table.

YOUTUBE

Eligibility, not income

YouTube's Partner Program is available in Nigeria, but creators still have to meet thresholds (500 subs for entry-level access, higher for ad revenue) before earning anything meaningful.1 Most of our nanos aren't there yet.

META

Open — but eligibility-based

Meta opened in-stream ads and Reels monetization to Nigerian creators, but it's still eligibility-based — not automatic income for everyone.2

TIKTOK

No Creator Rewards in Nigeria

As of late 2025, Nigeria was not in TikTok's Creator Rewards Program. Nigerian TikTok creators earn from brand partnerships, live gifts, affiliate, services and product sales — not views.3

What this means for us

Creators will say yes when we offer guaranteed payment, relevant briefs, simple deliverables, fast payout, repeat access — and we don't make them look bad in front of their audience. That's the actual creator pitch.

Realistic Nigerian fee bands

Rates vary by niche, city, deliverables, usage rights. These are starting test bands from an industry source4, mapped to who we should target.

Creator type Who we target Our test fee

Our target band: strong nano + lower micro. Good content, real engagement, not yet expensive, hungry for structured brand work. We do not chase 100k+ creators — they're not our early market.

Chapter 03 / The pricing math

Why ₦100k breaks us.

Daniel, this is the part you flagged when we last talked. Let me walk through the math so we both see it the same way.

The ₦100k breakdown

SME pays ₦100,000. We take 20% = ₦20k. Creator pool = ₦80k. Split across 5 creators = ₦16k each. That gets us weak creators, weak videos, late delivery and a disappointed SME. Not a business.

The three price floors I'm proposing

₦100k
Content test

Never call this a campaign. It buys 1–2 creator videos, no posting guarantee, no heavy reporting. Mostly for us to learn, not to make money on.

₦250k – ₦300k
Our minimum

The lowest price at which we can pay creators properly, run the campaign, track results, write the report, and still keep margin. Anyone below this number gets a content test, not a campaign.

₦500k+
Sweet spot

5–7 creators, full reporting, usage rights, optional ad boost. This is where we make real margin and where the model becomes repeatable. The first 5 paid campaigns should aim here.

The rule I want us to enforce: if an SME says "we only have ₦100k", we don't squeeze a 5-creator campaign into it. We offer them the content test instead. If they want a real campaign, the price floor is ₦250k. We hold this line on every call.

Chapter 05 / Us vs. ads

TikTok ads buy distribution.
We give them creator content,
local trust, execution and a report.

— How I want us to answer the "why not just ads?" question
What ads do

TikTok Ads Manager lets a business set objectives, target audiences, set budgets and run creative.5 It's a system to spend money on distribution. That's it.

What ads don't do

Ads do not source creators, write briefs, review content, manage revisions, confirm posting, or write a report. SMEs still need good creative — bad creative + paid ads is just faster money burning.

TikTok itself agrees

Their own small-business guide pushes an "always-engaged" model: organic posts + paid campaigns + creator partnerships, with Spark Ads to boost organic creator content into paid.6

Where we fit

We are the creator-content + execution layer. Our Package 03 combines creator campaign + reusable content + optional ad boost — so SMEs don't have to choose between us and ads.

Payout structure

How we pay creators.

Samuel — when you brief creators, this is the model. No view-based payouts. Fixed fees on delivery, plus capped bonuses for the few signals that actually matter to the business.

Component Use it? Why
Fixed content fee Yes Predictable, fair, fast to settle. The base of every brief.
Posting confirmation fee Yes Locks in proof of delivery before we release the bonus pool.
View-based payout No Too easy to manipulate. This is exactly where the old fraud problem lived.
Sales / coupon-code bonus Yes — capped Tied to real business value. Cap per campaign so we never blow margin.
Repeat-creator bonus Yes Rewards reliability. Builds the Trusted tier on Samuel's bench.
Product sample Yes Beauty, food, fashion especially. Lets us soften cash on small budgets.
Pure barter (no cash) Maybe Some nanos accept it. We won't rely on it alone — and never with mid-tier.
The creator pitch (Samuel — use this line)

"TrendStack gives vetted creators paid brand briefs. Fixed fee for approved content and posting. Trusted creators get bonuses and repeat campaign access. You don't have to stop your normal content — these are paid briefs you take when they fit your audience."

Chapter 06 / My verdict

Two paths. One trigger.

After both tests, here's how I want us to decide. The trigger is the SME side: if budgets land where I think they should, we run the influencer model. If they don't, we don't force it — we pivot to UGC-for-ads, which fits SME budgets better.

Path A · run it

If we can sell 5 campaigns at ₦250k–₦750k, pay creators properly, keep at least 25% margin, and get 2 businesses interested in repeating — the model works.

Decision Continue the managed creator campaign model. Tighten the bench. Push Package 02 → Package 03 conversions. Build the case study.
Mixed signal · narrow

Some SMEs pay ₦250k. Most balk. Creators accept fees but delivery is shaky. Margin is real but thin.

Decision Narrow to one vertical where Package 02 keeps closing. Drop the others for now. Tighten the creator bench. Re-test in 30 days.
Path B · pivot

SMEs only want to pay ₦50k–₦100k. Creators want ₦50k–₦150k each. The math doesn't close. The influencer model is wrong for this market right now.

Decision Pivot to UGC content for ads. Creators make videos, SME posts and boosts them through TikTok / Meta ads. Cheaper for the SME, simpler for us, fits the budget reality.
The validation target

5 campaigns at ₦250k–₦750k. 25% margin floor. 2 repeat conversations. That's the bar. If we hit it inside 90 days, we keep building. If we miss it badly, we pivot — no ego.
