Chapter 01 / Six findings

If we read nothing else.

Six takeaways. Everything below is the math and evidence that got us here.

01 · Creators will play

Yes — the right ones.

Platform pay in Nigeria is patchy. Most nano and lower-micro creators aren't earning real platform money yet. They'll take a fair ₦25k–₦80k gig from us. The 100k+ creators won't — and we shouldn't chase them.

02 · ₦100k isn't a campaign

It's a content test.

₦100k with 20% margin = ₦16k per creator across five creators. That buys bad work and a disappointed SME. ₦100k only ever buys 1–2 videos. Never a campaign.

03 · Our floor is ₦250k

₦500k+ is the sweet spot.

Below ₦250k–₦300k we can't pay creators, run the campaign, track results, write the report and keep margin. ₦500k is where the model becomes repeatable.

04 · SMEs will pay

Not as an ad replacement.

They pay us for creator content, local trust, execution and a report — what ads alone don't give them. We position as complementary to TikTok/IG ads, not a cheaper alternative.

05 · Fixed fees, no views

Pay on completion.

Fixed content fee + posting confirmation + capped sales/code bonus + repeat-creator bonus. No view-based payouts — that's where fraud lives. Product samples count for beauty, food and fashion.

06 · The pivot clause

If SMEs only have ₦100k, we pivot.

If our 30-SME test shows budgets stuck at ₦50k–₦100k, we don't force the influencer model. We pivot to UGC content for ads: creators make videos, the SME boosts them through TikTok / Meta ads. Cheaper for them, cleaner for us.

Chapter 02 / Will creators play?

Platform money doesn't kill us.

Our biggest worry. Here's the honest picture from public sources on Nigerian creators. Platform monetization raises the bar for what we offer creators — it doesn't take them off the table.

YOUTUBE

Eligibility, not income

Partner Program is open in Nigeria but creators need 500 subs for entry-level access and higher thresholds for ad revenue.1 Most of our nanos aren't there yet.

META

Open — eligibility-based

Meta opened in-stream ads and Reels monetization to Nigerian creators. Still eligibility-based — not automatic income.2

TIKTOK

No Creator Rewards in Nigeria

As of late 2025, Nigeria isn't in TikTok's Creator Rewards Program. Nigerian creators earn from brand deals, live gifts, affiliate, services and product sales.3

What this means for us

Creators say yes when we offer guaranteed payment, relevant briefs, simple deliverables, fast payout, repeat access — and we don't make them look bad to their audience. That's our pitch.

Chapter 03 / The pricing math

Why ₦100k breaks us.

The math we all need to see the same way.

The ₦100k breakdown

SME pays ₦100,000. We take 20% = ₦20k. Creator pool = ₦80k. Split across 5 creators = ₦16k each. Weak creators, weak videos, late delivery, disappointed SME. Not a business.

Our three price floors

₦100k
Content test

Never a campaign. Buys 1–2 creator videos, no posting guarantee, no heavy reporting. Mostly for learning.

₦250k – ₦300k
Our minimum

Lowest price at which we pay creators properly, run the campaign, track results, write the report and keep margin. Below this = content test, not campaign.

₦500k+
Sweet spot

5–7 creators, full reporting, usage rights, optional ad boost. Real margin lives here. The first 5 paid campaigns should aim for this band.

The rule we hold on every call: if an SME says "we only have ₦100k", we offer the content test — not a 5-creator campaign squeezed in. Real campaigns start at ₦250k.

Chapter 05 / Us vs. ads

TikTok ads buy distribution.
We give them creator content,
local trust, execution and a report.

— How we answer the "why not just ads?" question
What ads do

TikTok Ads Manager lets a business set objectives, target audiences, set budgets and run creative.5 A system for spending on distribution.

What ads don't do

Ads don't source creators, write briefs, review content, manage revisions, confirm posting or write a report. SMEs still need good creative — bad creative + paid ads is just faster money burning.

TikTok itself agrees

Their own small-business guide pushes organic posts + paid campaigns + creator partnerships, with Spark Ads to boost organic creator content into paid.6

Where we fit

We are the creator-content + execution layer. Package 03 combines creator campaign + reusable content + optional ad boost — so SMEs don't have to choose between us and ads.

Payout structure

How we pay creators.

The model we use in every brief. No view-based payouts. Fixed fees on delivery, plus capped bonuses on the signals that matter to the business.

Component Use it? Why
Fixed content fee Yes Predictable, fair, fast to settle. The base of every brief.
Posting confirmation fee Yes Locks in proof of delivery before we release the bonus pool.
View-based payout No Too easy to manipulate. This is exactly where our old fraud problem lived.
Sales / coupon-code bonus Yes — capped Tied to real business value. Capped per campaign so we never blow margin.
Repeat-creator bonus Yes Rewards reliability. Builds our Trusted tier.
Product sample Yes Beauty, food, fashion especially. Lets us soften cash on small budgets.
Pure barter (no cash) Maybe Some nanos accept it. We never rely on it alone — and never with mid-tier.
Our pitch to creators

"TrendStack gives vetted creators paid brand briefs. Fixed fee for approved content and posting. Trusted creators get bonuses and repeat campaign access. You don't have to stop your normal content — these are paid briefs you take when they fit your audience."

Validation

Before we commit. Two tests.

Two short tests in the next 14 days before we lock pricing — one on the creator side, one on the SME side.

Test 01 · Creator side

Speak to 30 creators. Same script.

"What is the lowest fee you would accept for one 30-second brand video posted on your TikTok or Instagram, with one revision and payment after posting?"

Capture per creator

  1. Lowest acceptable fee
  2. Preferred platform
  3. Accepts product + cash mix?
  4. Allows usage rights?
  5. Can deliver in 5 days?
  6. Has done brand work before?
Test 02 · SME side

Speak to 30 SMEs. Show all three packages.

"Which of these would you pay for this month?" — not "would you be interested." Force a real choice.

Show these three

  1. Starter Content Test — ₦100k–₦150k · 1–2 creator videos
  2. Nano Campaign — ₦250k–₦300k · 3–5 nano creators
  3. Growth Campaign — ₦500k–₦750k · 5–8 creators + report
Chapter 06 / Our verdict

Two paths. One trigger.

How we decide after both tests. The trigger is the SME side: if budgets land where we think they should, we run the influencer model. If they don't, we pivot to UGC-for-ads — which fits SME budgets better.

Path A · run it

We close 5 campaigns at ₦250k–₦750k, pay creators properly, keep 25% margin, and get 2 businesses interested in repeating. The model works.

Decision Continue the managed creator campaign model. Tighten the bench. Push Package 02 → Package 03 conversions. Build the case study.
Mixed signal · narrow

Some SMEs pay ₦250k. Most balk. Creators accept fees but delivery is shaky. Margin is real but thin.

Decision Narrow to one vertical where Package 02 keeps closing. Drop the others. Tighten the creator bench. Re-test in 30 days.
Path B · pivot

SMEs only want to pay ₦50k–₦100k. Creators want ₦50k–₦150k each. The math doesn't close. The influencer model is wrong for this market right now.

Decision Pivot to UGC content for ads. Creators make videos, SME posts and boosts via TikTok / Meta ads. Cheaper for the SME, simpler for us, fits the budget reality.
The validation target

5 campaigns at ₦250k–₦750k. 25% margin floor. 2 repeat conversations. That's the bar. Hit it inside 90 days, we keep building. Miss it badly, we pivot — no ego.
